Meet the Team

The AccessSTEM team is made up of 8 passionate and dedicated high school students from R.E. Mountain Secondary School. Each member brings their own unique skills, experiences, and enthusiasm for STEM education, and we're all committed to creating a fun, welcoming, and inclusive environment for our campers. Whether it's leading stations, designing activities, managing outreach, or supporting the registration process, every team member plays an important role in making AccessSTEM a success. We can't wait to share our love of STEM with our campers and help them discover new interests and make lasting friendships!

Outreach & Materials Lead

Daniel Soloshchenko

Hi! I'm Daniel, a Grade 11 IB student at R.E. Mountain Secondary School.

I've always loved figuring out how things work, whether it's cars, airplanes, robots, or anything mechanical. As President of our Robotics Club and a Senior Executive of the Debate Club, I enjoy solving problems and helping others learn new skills.

Growing up with four younger siblings has given me lots of experience working with kids (and plenty of patience!). Over the years, I've loved introducing them to science and helping them explore their interests. I also have two years of professional cooking experience and hold a FoodSafe Certification. At AccessSTEM, I'm excited to create fun activities and help campers discover that STEM can be exciting, creative, and accessible to everyone.

Camp Director

Hanna Yoo

Hi! I'm Hanna, a Grade 11 IB student at R.E. Mountain Secondary School and the founder of AccessSTEM.

I've always been passionate about STEM and believe that every child should have the opportunity to explore science, technology, engineering, and math in a supportive environment. I've worked with children as a camp counselor at an English camp in Japan for students in Grades 3-5 and as a classroom mentor.

I'm CPR certified, FoodSafe certified, and involved in both Robotics and Physics club. I also have a bunny named Boba! I started AccessSTEM because I wanted to create a camp where every child feels welcome, regardless of their background or experience level. My goal is to help students build confidence, make new friends, and discover that STEM is for everyone, not just a certain type of person.
